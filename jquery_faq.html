Who is the director of the National Park Service?

Shawn Benge, Deputy Director of Operations

What government agency oversees the National Park Service?

The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior.

How many areas are in the National Park System?

423 areas covering more than 85 million acres

What is the largest national park site?

Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres

What is the smallest national park site?

Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres

How many people visit the national parks?

Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2019: 327,516,619.

How many employees are in the National Park Service?

Approximately 20,000

How old is the National Park System?

The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.

What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?

The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior

What is the National Park Service budget?

$2.932 billion in fiscal year 2017

Wrangell-St. Elias National Park

  • Largest national park in the United States.
  • Largest glaciers in North America.
  • Resources in the park contributed to Allied victory in World War I.
  • Only two public roadways in the entire park.

Death Valley National Park

  • Lowest point in North America.
  • Temperatures of up to 127 degrees fahrenheit
  • Large rocks can move on their own for hundreds of feet.
  • Home to the Roadrunner.

Yellowstone National Park

  • Half the world’s hydrothermal features are found at Yellowstone.
  • Bison roam everywhere including roadways.
  • Yellowstone’s history dates back 11,000 years.
  • One of the worlds largest active supervolcanoes.
